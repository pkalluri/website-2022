I am a PhD student at the Stanford Artificial Intelligence Laboratory, advised by Stefano Ermon and Dan Jurafsky.
Here I pursue two lines of research:
  1. I am interested in discovering and inducing semantic concepts inside machine learning models. This leads me to work on interpretability and novel learning objectives that incentivize cleaner representations.
  2. I am interested in (and started a small virtual research community) examining how machine learning models shift power, and exploring the radical hope that our communities can build radical AI/human systems that shift power back to the people.
Previously, I completed my bachelors in computer science at MIT (2016), advised by Patrick Winston, in the lineage of Marvin Minsky, and I was a visiting researcher at Complutense University of Madrid (2017), advised by Pablo Gervas. I have received the NSF Graduate Research Fellowship, the Open Philanthropy AI Fellowship, the Paul and Daisy Soros Fellowship, the Stanford EDGE Fellowship, and the Interact Fellowship. I am also a writer and poet, grateful to have received mentorship from Junot Díaz and Charif Shanahan.
