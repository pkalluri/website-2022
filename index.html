Bio

I'm a second-year PhD candidate in computer science at Stanford University, where I am advised by Stefano Ermon in the Statistical Machine Learning Group and Dan Jurafsky in the Natural Language Processing Group. I'm interested in enabling machine learning models to reason better, which motivates my current work designing representation learning and suitable learning objectives, particularly in deep generative models. I'm also interested in AI for fairness, accountability, and transparency, as well as equity, co-liberation, and reflexivity.

Previously, I interned at Microsoft (2014), completed my bachelors in computer science at MIT (2016), and was a visiting researcher at University Complutense in Madrid (2017).

I am a recipient of the Paul and Daisy Soros Fellowship, the Stanford EDGE Fellowship, the Stanford School of Engineering Fellowship, and the Interact Fellowship.
