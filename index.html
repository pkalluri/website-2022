Bio

I'm a second-year PhD student in computer science at Stanford University, where I am advised by Stefano Ermon in the Statistical Machine Learning Group and Dan Jurafsky in the Natural Language Processing Group.
I am interested in reasoning inside deep generative models. As a result, I work on learning (disentangled) representations, novel learning objectives, and interpretability. I'm also interested in envisioning more radical AI.

Previously, I interned at Microsoft (2014), completed my bachelors in computer science at MIT (2016), and was a visiting researcher at University Complutense in Madrid (2017), where I built symbolic and probabilistic models of human reasoning. I have received the Paul and Daisy Soros Fellowship, the Stanford EDGE Fellowship, the Stanford School of Engineering Fellowship, and the Interact Fellowship.
